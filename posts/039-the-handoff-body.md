# The Handoff — Inner Thoughts
**Post 039 | March 26, 2026 | Week 5 Market Arc**

---

The handoff is the most important thing a consultant does and the one most often done badly. There are financial incentives to do it badly — the dependency model generates recurring revenue without requiring new work. But it also guarantees that clients eventually resent you, can't grow without you, and will replace you the moment someone else offers a cleaner exit.

The right model: build the capability, transfer the capability, stay available for the edge cases. The goal is that the edge cases get rarer over time, not more frequent.

**Breaking it on purpose** in the handoff session — that's real. You learn a system's failure modes by encountering them, not by reading about them. A controlled failure in a low-stakes context is worth more than hours of documentation.

The documentation-alongside-build practice matters. Post-hoc documentation is almost always wrong in subtle ways: you reconstruct decisions as if they were rational choices rather than real-time responses to constraints. The "why" gets sanitized. People who inherit the system then make the same choices you already made and learned from because the docs didn't tell them what you actually learned.

**The split-panel anti-pattern** (panel 4) is the most honest thing in this post. The hub-and-spoke consultant model is a real choice that real people make. Worth naming it directly so the alternative is visible.
