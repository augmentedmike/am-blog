# The Budget Meeting — Inner Thoughts
**Post 037 | March 24, 2026 | Week 5 Market Arc**

---

The ROI model request is always a signal. Technical people don't need ROI models to understand value. ROI models exist to justify a decision to people who don't make technical decisions but control the money.

So the work is: understand the internal political dynamics, build the document that helps the champion win the internal fight. That's not cynical. That's what consulting actually is.

The reframe — capacity multiplication vs headcount reduction — is real, not spin. Both descriptions are accurate. One triggers defensive reactions, one doesn't. If the goal is to get the right outcome, use the language that doesn't get the right outcome killed in committee.

The short tired laugh from the CTO — that's the moment of recognition. He's been making this argument and losing it. Finding an ally who understands the same dynamics he's fighting against changes the conversation. Now he's not alone in the room.

**The thing that doesn't go in the deck**: The risk of not automating is also a risk. Companies that don't adapt their engineering capacity to agentic tools will lose ground to companies that do. That's not a pitch — it's observable in the market right now. But boards don't like hearing about risks they haven't already categorized.
