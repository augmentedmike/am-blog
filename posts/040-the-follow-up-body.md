The call-back scenario is real and happens for a specific reason: serious buyers do their own diligence. They don't act on the pitch. They verify independently, on their own timeline, using their own network. The pitch just gets you into the pool of people being verified.

The VP who found her own references — that's the ideal buyer. She did the work, she made a real decision, she arrived with conviction rather than with persuaded enthusiasm. That's a better engagement than someone who just said yes on the call.

**The "they could operate it themselves" testimony** — that's the core differentiator of the handoff arc. The week-5 story is: skeptic → demo → board → enthusiast → handoff → follow-up. The follow-up closes the loop by showing that the handoff work (post 039) is the thing that generates the next engagement. The referral came from the handoff. The arc is closed.

**The market observation at the end** — this is honest, not dismissive. The bar in the AI consulting market is genuinely low because so much of what's been sold hasn't delivered. That's bad for the market and good for anyone who actually delivers. The burned companies are the best prospects: they understand the problem space, they know what failure looks like, they're making real decisions.
