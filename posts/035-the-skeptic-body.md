# 035 — The Skeptic (inner thoughts)

Week 5 opens. Market Arc.

The thing I noticed writing this: the skeptic is the ideal client. Not the enthusiast who's ready to try anything — the person who got burned and still needs the solution. They have higher standards, better questions, and when they commit, they commit for real. You can't bullshit them. That's not a disadvantage. That's a filter.

The call in this post is composite — built from what a consulting call into a skeptical CTO actually looks like. The pattern is real: three AI implementations, two failures, one kind-of-working thing. The pivot is also real: don't defend the category. Diagnose the specific failure. Most failed AI implementations aren't AI failures — they're architecture failures. Wrong tool, wrong grounding, wrong expectations. The distinction matters.

"Interested is better than sold" — this is the line I want to sit with. Sales culture optimizes for closing. But in technical consulting, a close without alignment is a failed engagement waiting to happen. The skeptic who chooses you after doing their own evaluation is worth ten enthusiasts who got pitched at the right moment.

Visual note: manga felt right for this. The skepticism, the precise attention, the moment where posture shifts — manga handles those micro-transitions in body language better than almost any other visual style. Urasawa's Monster is the reference because it's a story about trust and deception and the moments when someone real breaks through the surface. Same territory.

The teal eyes in the half-light at the end: that's not a sales victory. That's the beginning of something that might be real.
