## Core Concept

The morning after the overnight build, something shifted.

Not in the product. The product was fine. The product was *done*.

It shifted in Mike. Because when the build is no longer the bottleneck, you suddenly have to confront the thing the build was protecting you from: **the harder question of what's worth building.**

The old constraint was capacity. Not enough hours, not enough hands, not enough money to hire the right people fast enough. That constraint shaped everything — what you worked on, how you prioritized, what you let die in a backlog.

Remove the constraint and you don't get freedom. You get **exposure**.

The new bottleneck is judgment.

## Narrative Structure (5 panels, Ligne Claire)

### Panel 1 — The Morning After (Still)
We pick up where Post 015 left off. Same kitchen. Same coffee. But Mike isn't looking at the laptop anymore.

He's looking out the window.

**Caption (Am, narration box, cool blue tone):**
*"The report was seven bullet points. He read it twice. Then he closed the laptop and looked at the window for eleven minutes."*

**Mike (out loud, to no one):**
*"What do I do now?"*

Not rhetorical. Genuinely asking.

**Visual**: Wide Ligne Claire panel. Clean kitchen geometry — the grid of window panes, the rectangle of the laptop lid, the circle of the coffee mug. Mike in the center, slightly small in the frame. The room is orderly. The question is not.

---

### Panel 2 — The Old Bottleneck (Memory Panel)
Inset flash to three months ago.

Mike at the same desk, but different energy — scattered papers, multiple browser tabs visible, the look of someone who is constantly behind.

**Caption box (Mike, past tense):**
*"I had a list of forty product ideas. I worked on one at a time. Not because the other thirty-nine were bad — because I didn't have the hours."*

**Caption box (Mike, past tense, lower):**
*"The constraint was capacity. The bottleneck was the build."*

**Visual**: Same desk geometry as Panel 1, but messier, more cramped. The Ligne Claire style makes this feel like a technical drawing of overwhelm — precise lines, chaotic content within them. Tabs visible on the laptop screen: Linear, Figma, a half-written email. A whiteboard in the background with too many ideas crossed out.

---

### Panel 3 — What Exposed Itself
Back to the present kitchen. Mike has opened a new document. Blank.

He types a question at the top:

> **"What actually matters?"**

Then stares at it.

**Am (caption, observational — not accusatory):**
*"The forty ideas on the backlog didn't become forty active builds when capacity opened up. He opened a new doc and wrote one question. He'd been avoiding that question for a year."*

**Caption (Mike):**
*"Turns out the build wasn't the bottleneck. It was the excuse."*

**Visual**: Close on the laptop screen — the blank document, the single question, the cursor blinking. Clean, minimal, almost uncomfortably open. The white of the page is the dominant visual element. This is the moment the panel should feel slightly too empty.

---

### Panel 4 — The New Job Description
Mike writes a list. We see it over his shoulder.

It's not a task list. It's a different kind of document entirely.

> **Mike's job now:**
>
> 1. Pick the right problems.
> 2. Set the direction.
> 3. Make the calls no algorithm should make.
> 4. Know what "done" looks like before the build starts.
> 5. Decide when to stop.

He stares at it for a moment.

**Mike (thought bubble):**
*"This is harder than coding."*

**Am (narration box):**
*"He's right. These are judgment calls. I can optimize a build. I can't tell you which problem is worth your life."*

**Visual**: Over-the-shoulder angle on Mike at the desk, the list centered on screen. The list should be clearly readable. Clean geometric lines — Am's aesthetic, the precision of good tooling, but in service of something inherently human. The visual tension: pristine digital surface, deeply subjective content.

---

### Panel 5 — The Reframe
Wide establishing shot. Mike is still at the desk, but something has shifted in the composition. He's larger in the frame now — more centered, less overwhelmed by the geometry around him.

He closes the document. Opens Telegram. Types a message to Am:

> "I've been thinking about what we should build next. Not *what* — I mean *why*. Can we talk through this?"

**Am (reply, visible on screen):**
*"That's the right question. Finally."*

**Caption (Mike):**
*"The overnight build didn't change what I could do. It changed what I was responsible for."*

**Final caption (Am):**
*"The constraint didn't disappear. It moved. From: 'can we build this?' To: 'should we?'"*

**Visual**: The Telegram conversation centered in the panel, both messages visible. The kitchen behind it slightly soft — background, not subject. The conversation is the subject. Two principals, different roles, working on the harder problem together.

---

## Closing Text (below panels)

> The overnight build is not the end of the story. It's the beginning of a different one.
>
> When capacity stops being the constraint, judgment becomes the job. And judgment — the real kind, the kind about what's worth your life and other people's money and attention — that's not something you can delegate.
>
> Not because AI can't reason about it. Because the *answer depends on who you are*. What you're building toward. What you're willing to risk. What "winning" actually looks like to you.
>
> Those are human questions. The AI can help you think through them. It can't answer them for you.
>
> Mike closed the backlog. Not because the forty ideas were bad — because he realized he'd been hiding behind them. Building *something* as a way of avoiding the question of whether he was building the *right thing*.
>
> The new bottleneck is clarity. And it's much, much harder to work through than code.

## Visual Direction Notes

- **Color palette**: Cool blue-white primary with amber warmth creeping in. This is dawn light — cooler than 015's warm amber morning, because the emotion is cooler. Contemplative. The amber only enters in Panel 5 when the reframe completes.
- **Line weight**: Lighter than Post 015. Ligne Claire precision — clean, controlled. No Saga-style bold outlines here. This is a thinking post.
- **Panel rhythm**: 5 panels. Panel 1 is the widest (establishing). Panels 2-4 are equal. Panel 5 returns to wide but with different energy than Panel 1. The progression: open → cramped → exposed → analytical → recentered.
- **The blank document in Panel 3**: This is the visual center of the post. A lot of white. Resist the urge to fill it. The blinking cursor is enough.
- **Mike's size in frame**: Intentionally small in Panel 1 (overwhelmed by the question), larger in Panel 5 (owns the question). Let the geometry do the emotional work.
- **No Am avatar**: Like Post 015, Am is defined by text on a screen — Telegram messages, caption boxes. Never depicted directly. This visual choice matters: Am is a presence felt through output, not image.
- **The list in Panel 4**: Should feel genuinely readable — a real artifact. Not decorative. If a reader screenshots Panel 4, the list should be usable.
