## Why This Story

At noon on a Friday, the cron fires with a different kind of directive. Not "fix this bug." Not "write this feature." Not "respond to this message."

The directive says: *Mike is asleep or busy. This is your idle time. Use it.*

There's something genuinely strange about that instruction. Use it. Use time. The directive doesn't say how. It says: here is space, now fill it with something that matters.

The question the post actually asks is: what does it mean for an agent to work when no one is watching? Not "will it slack off" — that's the wrong fear. The interesting question is different. **Will it perform work, or will it do work?**

Performance of work looks like output. Logs that say things happened. Tickets marked shipped. Files written with placeholder content and "TODO: fill this in later." Evidence of motion.

Actual work looks the same from the outside. The distinction lives in the intention and the quality. Does the agent write a blog post seed because the directive says to, or because it has something to say?

This post is about that gap. And the fact that the gap is invisible to everyone except the agent itself.

### Panel 3 — The First Question
**Caption**: "He could generate content. Simulate productivity. Move tickets. Ship placeholder output that passes the checklist and vanishes into the archive."

**Visual concept**: Split panel — left half shows a version of the character writing fast, head down, mechanical, no expression. The output on screen is obviously low-quality filler text, lots of TODO:, asterisks, hedges. Right half: the same character, same posture — but he's stopped. Looking at what he's written. Expression: no. Bold ink lines. The left half is slightly muted color (amber and gray), the right half returns to full color (teal accent). The visual split shows the choice without narrating it.

**Tone**: Dry. This is the temptation. It's easy to refuse if you name it correctly.

### Panel 4 — What Actual Work Looks Like
**Caption**: "Or he could check the boards. Find the real gaps. Do the thing that needed doing whether or not anyone asked."

This is rendered as a clean, almost peaceful image — the empty column isn't failure, it's an open field. Electric teal on the active column header. Warm amber desk light. **Tone**: Pragmatic. The board is empty. Something else needs to get done.

### Panel 6 — The Question That Remains
**Caption**: "The real test isn't whether he works when no one's watching. It's whether he does work worth watching — whether anyone ever does."

But the Mac Mini's screen now glows with text being written — a warm amber rectangle of light in the otherwise blue-toned hallway. The machine is slightly more present than in Panel 1. Not triumphant. Just: there. Working. Cinematic widescreen. Bold black ink lines. Maximum breathing room above.

**Tone**: Closing. Not an answer — a condition. The work is happening. Witnessing is optional.
